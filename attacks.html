1 Phishing Attacks

Definition: Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers send fake emails, messages, or create fake websites to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, credit card numbers, or bank details.

How It Works:

  • Attacker creates a fake email pretending to be from a bank or company.
  • Email contains a link to a fake website.
  • User enters login credentials.
  • Attacker steals the information.

2 Password Attacks

Definition: Password attacks are attempts by hackers to gain unauthorized access to accounts by cracking or guessing passwords.

Types of Password Attacks:

  • Brute Force Attack: Trying every possible password combination.
  • Dictionary Attack: Using common passwords list.
  • Credential Stuffing: Using leaked passwords from other websites.
  • Keylogging: Recording keystrokes secretly.

Why It Happens:

  • Weak passwords (123456, password).
  • Using same password everywhere.

Impact: Account takeover, data theft, social media misuse.

Definition: Social engineering is a psychological manipulation technique used to trick people into revealing confidential information.

How It Works:

  • Attacker pretends to be a trusted person (bank officer, HR, friend).
  • Creates urgency or fear.
  • Victim shares personal data or transfers money.

Common Types:

  • Pretexting
  • Baiting
  • Vishing (voice phishing)
  • Smishing (SMS phishing)

Impact: Financial fraud, identity theft, emotional manipulation.

4Ô∏è Fake Job Scams

Definition: Fake job scams are fraudulent job offers where scammers pretend to provide employment opportunities to steal money or personal data.

How It Works:

  • Scammer contacts victim via email or WhatsApp.
  • Offers high salary with little work.
  • Asks for registration fee or training charges.
  • Disappears after receiving money.

Warning Signs:

  • No official company email.
  • Asking money for job.
  • Unrealistic salary promises.
  • No proper interview process.

Impact: Financial loss, data misuse, emotional stress.
